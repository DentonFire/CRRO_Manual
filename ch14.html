Chapter 14

Quarterly Program Appraisals

14.1 Overview

Quarterly Program Appraisals are formal reports that document the full scope of CRR and Recruitment program activities, metrics, budget status, and strategic context for each fiscal quarter. These reports serve multiple purposes: accountability to department leadership, accreditation evidence, and institutional documentation.

Timing: End of each fiscal quarter — Q1: December 31, Q2: March 31, Q3: June 30, Q4: September 30

Fiscal Year: October 1 through September 30

Current: FY 25-26 (October 2025 – September 2026\) [UPDATE ANNUALLY]

14.2 Report Structure

The quarterly report follows a 10-section structure with appendices:

Cover Page

DFD branding, report title, quarter designation, date range, author name and title. Include checkboxes for appraisal type (Annual, Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4).

Program Snapshot

High-level summary including staff, vehicles (FD1865), annual budget ($36,200), and YTD expenditures.

Department Goals/Objectives

Reference the four Strategic Plan goals and Division Objectives (these remain constant across quarters):

  • Goal 1: Hire new recruits per Chapter 143 and Meet and Confer Agreement
  • Goal 2: Fire Academy and Paramedic Program visits
  • Goal 3: Develop CRR Plan following NFPA 1300
  • Goal 4: Review Public Education programs per Standards of Cover/CPSE
  • Division Objectives: Reduce risk through education; recruit and retain diverse workforce

Inputs/Resources

Financial table showing all four budget accounts with allocation, YTD spend, remaining balance, and percent spent. Also list physical resources (vehicle, equipment, social media accounts, drone equipment, PAT materials, recruiting materials) and essential resources.

Community Outreach Table

Quarterly metrics for all public education event categories:

Recruiting Table

Same format tracking fire academy visits, paramedic schools, virtual events, in-person events, and waitlist/newsletter subscriber count.

CONDITIONS

Narrative assessment of the current state of each program area: Recruiting Program, Public Education and Community Outreach, Technology and Tools Development, DFR/UAS Program, Scout/Explorer Program, Social Media and Communications. Include challenges, external factors, and context for the quarter's performance.

ACTIONS

Detailed accomplishments organized by category: Recruiting Accomplishments, Public Education Delivery, Technology and Tool Development, Social Media and Communication, DFR/UAS Program Development, Event Execution, Administrative Excellence, Infrastructure and Equipment. Use bullet points with specific deliverables.

NEEDS

Numbered list of identified needs, resource requests, and strategic priorities for the upcoming period. This section is forward-looking and should identify gaps, risks, and opportunities.

Appendix

Include links to all CRR tools, newsletter archive URLs (Constant Contact), and any supporting documentation produced during the quarter.

14.3 Compilation Process

  1. Pull all 13 weekly reports from the quarter
  1. Aggregate activities by category (Recruiting, CRR, Meetings, Admin)
  1. Calculate totals (events, attendees, expenses)
  1. Pull budget actuals from Emburse and reconcile
  1. Write CONDITIONS narrative assessing current state
  1. Write ACTIONS narrative with specific accomplishments
  1. Identify NEEDS for upcoming period
  1. Add appendices (newsletter links, tool URLs)
  1. Format with DFD branding (see Appendix C for brand standards)
  1. Review for accuracy, spell check, verify all figures
  1. Create PDF version for distribution

See the Power Automate Setup Guide for automated quarterly compilation workflow.

14.4 Quality Checklist

Before submitting each quarterly appraisal:

  • All dates and numbers verified against source data
  • Budget figures reconciled with Emburse records
  • Spell check completed
  • Consistent formatting throughout
  • Headers and page numbers correct
  • All newsletter archive links functional
  • All tool URLs verified accessible
  • PDF version created
