Chapter 5

Public Education Delivery

5.1 Event Categories and Tracking

All public education events are tracked in the ESO system under standardized categories. Each event record captures the date, location, event type, attendance count, and whether the CRR Officer personally attended. Events flow to SharePoint via Power Automate for inclusion in weekly reports.

5.2 Event Coordination Workflow

The following workflow outlines the standard process for coordinating and documenting a public education event:

  1. Receive request — Email, phone, or internal referral requesting DFD participation
  1. Evaluate and schedule — Assess alignment with CRR goals, check calendar availability, coordinate with Operations if on-duty crew support needed
  1. Prepare materials — Gather appropriate handouts, display materials, equipment. For fire truck presentations, coordinate with assigned company.
  1. Execute event — Attend and deliver educational content. Capture attendance count and photos if applicable.
  1. Log in ESO — Create ESO community event record with date, location, type, attendance, and participation status
  1. Report — Event automatically flows to SharePoint via Power Automate for weekly report inclusion

5.3 Recurring Programs

Brookdale Senior Living Presentations

Regular fire safety presentations are conducted at Brookdale senior living facilities on a recurring schedule. Content focuses on fall prevention, cooking safety, heating safety, and emergency evacuation procedures tailored for senior populations. Maintain the established schedule with facility activity directors.

School Career Day Presentations

Career day presentations at local schools serve dual purposes: fire safety education and long-term recruitment pipeline development. Recent schools include Guyer High School, Calhoun Middle School, and Strickland Middle School. Content includes career path information, department overview, equipment demonstrations, and fire safety messages. Coordinate scheduling through school administration contacts.

Fire Extinguisher Training

Hands-on fire extinguisher training sessions are provided to community groups, businesses, and organizations on request. Equipment is maintained and stored at the CRR office. Ensure adequate extinguisher supplies and training props are available before each session.

5.4 Fire Prevention Week

Fire Prevention Week (October) is the flagship annual CRR campaign. This week-long effort involves coordinated events, social media campaigns, newsletter content, and community engagement activities aligned with the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) annual theme. Planning should begin in August with event scheduling, material ordering, and content creation.

Key activities include school visits, station open houses, social media campaigns, and community events. Coordinate with MarComm for messaging consistency and Operations for crew availability at events.

5.5 Smoke Detector Program

DFD partners with the American Red Cross to provide and install free smoke detectors within Denton city limits. Community members request detectors through an online form on the city website. In Q1 FY 25-26, a targeted installation drive resulted in 50 detectors installed in homes that previously lacked working smoke detection.

This program represents a direct intervention that connects public education to measurable risk reduction outcomes — a key element for CPSE accreditation compliance (see Recommendation \#5 response). Promote through CRR newsletters and community events. Track installations for quarterly reporting.

5.6 Hoarding Awareness Campaign

In January-February 2025, the CRR Division produced a public awareness video following a series of hoarding-related fires and fire fatalities in the community. The video featured a multi-disciplinary perspective:

  • CRR Officer discussing the scope of the problem (hoarding contributes to approximately 25% of all preventable fire deaths)
  • Truck Captain addressing rescue complications
  • Engine Lieutenant explaining increased fire load and suppression challenges
  • Firefighter/Paramedic covering medical emergency access barriers

The campaign included a call to action for the department’s Free Home Safety Inspection program, directly connecting public education to an intervention service. This campaign demonstrates the department’s expansion beyond traditional fire safety topics into non-traditional risk areas.
