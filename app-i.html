Appendix I: Recruiting Annual Calendar

This calendar outlines the annual rhythm of firefighter recruitment and hiring activities. Unlike CRR programming which follows a predictable seasonal cycle, recruiting operates on a hiring cycle that may not align perfectly with the fiscal year. The cycle length depends on department staffing needs and Civil Service Commission scheduling.

This appendix is organized in two sections: (1) recurring activities that happen regardless of cycle timing, and (2) the hiring cycle timeline that plays out over approximately 6-9 months whenever a cycle is initiated. [UPDATE ANNUALLY]

I.1 Recurring Activities (Year-Round)

These activities happen continuously regardless of whether a hiring cycle is active:

Activity Frequency Details
Recruiting newsletter As needed during cycle "Ready to Serve" series via Constant Contact (3,960 subscribers as of Q1 FY 25-26)
NEOGOV maintenance Monthly Job posting management, applicant data, workflow stages
Handshake (UNT) Ongoing Maintain employer profile, post opportunities, message candidates
Career fair attendance 2-3 per year UNT, TWU, and community career fairs
Fire academy visits Quarterly LaGrone, NCTC, Weatherford — recruit near-graduation candidates
Diversity outreach Ongoing Targeted events, partnerships, community engagement
School/youth outreach As scheduled Middle school and high school career days — long-term pipeline
Budget tracking Monthly Monitor 320100.7826 (Diversity) and 320100.7726 (Physicals/Psych)
Ride-Along Program Ongoing HIPAA training required. Candidates experience shift operations firsthand.
Explorer/Scout Program Ongoing BSA partnership, youth development, future pipeline

I.2 Hiring Cycle Timeline

The following timeline describes the typical sequence of a firefighter hiring cycle from initiation to academy start date. Phases are listed in order with approximate timeframes. Adjust based on candidate volume and department needs.

Phase 1: Pre-Cycle Planning (8-12 weeks before exam)

Week Activity Owner Key Details
-12 to -10 Confirm hiring need Chief Hedges Number of positions, target academy date
-10 to -8 NEOGOV job posting CRR Officer + HR Draft posting, set application window, confirm requirements
-8 FPSI exam coordination CRR Officer Order exam materials, confirm exam date with FPSI and Civil Service Commission
-8 to -4 Recruiting push CRR Officer Newsletter blasts, social media, career fairs, academy visits, Handshake
-4 to -2 Registration monitoring CRR Officer Track applicant count via NEOGOV, push additional outreach if numbers are low
-1 Exam logistics CRR Officer Reserve venue, confirm proctors, prepare materials, candidate communication

Phase 2: Civil Service Exam

Activity Owner Key Details
Exam day CRR Officer + proctors Administer written exam per Civil Service rules. Check IDs, collect extra point documentation.
Score processing FPSI → CRR Officer FPSI scores exams and returns results. CRR Officer applies preference points.
Eligibility list CRR Officer Generate ranked list, apply tie-breaking criteria, distribute to Chief and Civil Service Commission.
Candidate notification CRR Officer Notify all candidates of results. Pass candidates move to PHS.

FY 25-26 Reference: Exam held January 10, 2026. 76 candidates registered, 40 passed.

Phase 3: Personal History Statement (PHS) + VerifEYE (4-8 weeks)

Week Activity Owner Key Details
1 PHS distribution CRR Officer Distribute PHS packet via NEOGOV. Set deadline (typically 2-3 weeks).
1-3 PHS collection and review CRR Officer Collect completed PHS packets. Review for completeness and red flags.
2-4 Pre-Test Questionnaire CRR Officer Distribute DFD Pre-Test Questionnaire. Review responses for VerifEYE test type determination.
2-6 VerifEYE processing CRR Officer Generate test links via Converus dashboard. Process candidates sequentially. See Appendix G.2 and VerifEYE Processing Guide.
3-6 Background investigations Investigations team Captains Roberts, Miller, Morton conduct background checks on PHS data.
4-8 Results and DQ processing CRR Officer Review VerifEYE results and background reports. Issue DQ letters as needed (cite specific Hiring Guidelines sections).

FY 25-26 Reference: PHS distributed January 2026. VerifEYE processing January-February 2026. Background investigations concurrent.

Phase 4: Physical Ability Test (PAT) (2-4 weeks)

Week Activity Owner Key Details
-4 Schedule Practice PAT CRR Officer ~1 month before Official PAT. Coordinate venue (Station 7), on-duty crews. No OT in FY 25-26.
-4 Practice PAT notification CRR Officer Email all eligible candidates with date, time, location.
-3 Practice PAT CRR Officer + crews Familiarization run. Record attendance. Not scored.
-1 Official PAT logistics CRR Officer Hire OT (budget permitting), brief instructors, inspect equipment. Minimum 7 staff for official event.
0 Official PAT CRR Officer + staff Administer PAT. Record all times. 6 events (see Ch 9.5 for details).
+1 Results processing CRR Officer Update PAT spreadsheet. Communicate results. Advance passers in NEOGOV.

FY 25-26 Reference: Practice PAT Feb 7, 2026 (27/40 attended). Official PAT Feb 28, 2026 (33 passed, 6 failed, 1 DNS). See Chapter 9.5 for full results.

Phase 5: Panel Interviews (1-2 weeks)

Activity Owner Key Details
Schedule interviews CRR Officer Coordinate availability of Chief-level interview panel and top 20 candidates.
Prepare materials CRR Officer Provide panel members with PHS packets and background investigator reports.
Conduct interviews Interview panel Structured interview process (developed with Chief Skipper). Score and rank candidates.
Compile results CRR Officer Aggregate interview scores, combine with prior rankings, generate final hire recommendation.

Phase 6: Conditional Offer → Appointment (2-4 weeks)

Activity Owner Key Details
Conditional Letter of Employment CRR Officer Issue conditional offer to selected candidates.
Medical exam CRR Officer → Concentra Schedule NFPA 1582 medical (~$825/candidate, 320100.7726).
Psych evaluation CRR Officer → Pepper Schedule psychological evaluation (~$215/candidate, 320100.7726).
Medical/psych clearance CRR Officer Receive and review clearance reports.
Appointment Letter CRR Officer Issue final appointment letter. Coordinate start date with Training division.

Phase 7: Closeout

Activity Owner Key Details
NEOGOV closeout CRR Officer Update all candidate statuses. Close requisition. Archive records.
Laserfiche filing CRR Officer + Stacy Fruge Transfer candidate files to Laserfiche for permanent records storage.
Budget reconciliation CRR Officer Reconcile all hiring-related expenses (physicals, psych, PAT equipment, OT).
After-action review CRR Officer Document lessons learned, timeline actual vs. planned, process improvements.
Metrics compilation CRR Officer Compile diversity data, pass rates, timeline metrics for quarterly appraisal.

I.4 Planning Notes

  • Budget timing matters. Medical and psych exams are the largest per-candidate costs. Ensure 320100.7726 has adequate funds before issuing conditional offers.
  • VerifEYE is sequential. Candidates are processed one at a time through the credibility assessment. Plan for 1-2 candidates per day during peak processing.
  • Weather contingency. Always identify backup dates for PAT events. FY 25-26 Practice PAT was delayed one week due to ice.
  • Communication is constant. Candidates expect regular updates throughout the process. Use the Recruiting newsletter and individual emails to keep candidates informed at each stage transition.
  • Diversity data should be captured and tracked throughout the process for quarterly reporting and CPSE accreditation compliance.
  • Eligibility list expiration. Under Civil Service rules, the eligibility list remains valid for a defined period. Track expiration dates and plan subsequent hiring cycles accordingly.
