Chapter 15

Accreditation Support and Pension Board Duties

15.1 CPSE/CFAI Accreditation Support

The CRR Officer plays a direct role in supporting the department's accreditation through the Commission on Professional Standards for Excellence (CPSE) and the Commission on Fire Accreditation International (CFAI).

CRR Officer's Accreditation Role

  • Prepare Program Appraisals that serve as accreditation evidence
  • Respond to specific accreditation recommendations (see Recommendation \#5 and \#6 responses)
  • Document program outcomes and connect them to measurable results
  • Maintain evidence files for training records, community outreach data, and program evaluations
  • Support self-assessment updates and data collection

Program Appraisal Framework for Accreditation

For each CRR and Recruitment program, the accreditation framework requires documentation of five components:

  1. Program Description — What the program does, who it serves
  1. Objectives — Specific, measurable goals
  1. Performance Measures — How success is tracked (KPIs)
  1. Data Analysis — Trend analysis, outcomes data
  1. Continuous Improvement — Actions taken, planned improvements

Active Recommendation Responses

The CRR Officer has prepared responses to two active accreditation recommendations:

Recommendation \#5: Focus public education on target hazard analysis, expand beyond fire and life safety, connect programs with outcomes. — Response documents the target hazard-based newsletter program, hoarding awareness campaign, smoke detector drive, and After the Fire resource as evidence of compliance.

Recommendation \#6: Perform workload analysis for CRR staffing. — Response documents the 31 distinct program areas managed by a single FTE, with detailed workload table and comparable agency staffing analysis.

See Appendix D for complete recommendation response documents.

15.2 CAAS Accreditation

DFD achieved perfect-score CAAS (Commission on Accreditation of Ambulance Services) accreditation in August 2025\. While CAAS accreditation primarily involves EMS operations and clinical standards, the CRR Officer should be aware of:

  • CAAS standards that relate to community education and public access
  • Maintaining evidence of community risk reduction activities
  • Supporting quality improvement initiatives when they overlap with CRR programs

15.3 Pension Board Trustee Duties

In addition to the primary CRR and Recruitment responsibilities, the CRR Officer position holder currently serves as a Trustee on the Denton Firemen's Relief and Retirement Fund (DFRRF). This is a separate fiduciary duty that carries personal legal obligations.

Fund Overview

Element Detail
Fund Name Denton Firemen's Relief and Retirement Fund (DFRRF)
Type Defined benefit pension plan
AUM \~$185 million
System Texas Local Firefighters Retirement Act (TLFFRA)
Primary Investment Manager Garnett Advisors

Trustee Responsibilities

  • Attend monthly board meetings
  • Review investment performance reports and compare to benchmarks
  • Participate in manager selection and evaluation
  • Maintain prudent investor standard
  • Document all decisions and rationale
  • Avoid conflicts of interest

Real Estate Sub-Committee

The CRR Officer currently serves on the Real Estate Sub-Committee, which provides oversight of approximately $20 million in direct property investments (DFP Properties, 4 properties). Committee responsibilities include:

  • Evaluating real estate investment performance
  • Considering the need for a dedicated real estate advisor as Garnett moves to a fully invested model
  • Meeting regularly (monthly or as needed) to review portfolio status

Active Pension Initiatives

  • Real Estate Advisor Evaluation — Consider dedicated RE advisor as portfolio transitions
  • Board Composition — Recruit Mayor's appointee with banking/lending expertise
  • Fee Optimization — Expense-neutral approach to new advisory relationships

Meeting Preparation

For each board meeting, the trustee should:

  1. Review prior meeting minutes
  1. Analyze monthly/quarterly performance reports
  1. Compare performance to benchmarks
  1. Prepare questions for investment managers
  1. Review any proposals or policy changes
  1. Note market conditions and trends

Note: Pension board service is a personal appointment, not inherently tied to the CRR Officer position. A successor may or may not serve on the pension board depending on department needs and trustee elections.
