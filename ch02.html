Chapter 2

Position Overview

2.1 Position Summary

The Community Risk Reduction Officer is a single-FTE position responsible for the full scope of both Community Risk Reduction/Public Education and Firefighter Recruitment/Hiring at the Denton Fire Department. This dual-mission role is unique within the department and carries sole responsibility for 31 distinct program areas spanning community outreach, public education delivery, newsletter production, technology tool development, the entire Civil Service hiring process, accreditation support, and administrative reporting.

The position also carries additional duties as a Pension Board Trustee for the Denton Firemen's Relief and Retirement Fund (\~$185M AUM), including Real Estate Sub-Committee membership, and must maintain personal firefighter/paramedic continuing education requirements (TCFP/DSHS certifications).

2.2 Primary Responsibilities

Community Risk Reduction (CRR)

  • Plan, coordinate, and deliver public education events (fire safety presentations, fire truck displays, station tours, fire extinguisher training)
  • Produce and distribute monthly CRR newsletters via Constant Contact
  • Develop and maintain web-based CRR operational tools (Water Shutoff Survey, Fire Watch Guidelines, Knox Box Guide, After the Fire)
  • Manage special programs: Citizens Fire Academy, Explorer/Scout Program, Clown Program, Fire Camp
  • Track and report community outreach metrics through ESO system
  • Coordinate with Fire Prevention, Emergency Management, and Operations on overlapping programs
  • Support CPSE/CAAS accreditation through program appraisals and recommendation responses

Firefighter Recruitment & Hiring

  • Generate annual Position Opportunity Announcement (POA) for Civil Service Commission approval
  • Coordinate all Civil Service exam logistics: test provider (FPSI), venue, materials, proctors
  • Manage the full NEOGOV hiring workflow from application through appointment
  • Verify candidate extra points (military service, residency, LaGrone Academy)
  • Certify eligibility list and present demographics report to Civil Service Commission
  • Coordinate each hiring stage: Personal History Statement, polygraph (VerifEYE), PAT, panel interviews, psych exam, medical exam
  • Generate all correspondence: disqualification letters, conditional letters of employment, appointment letters
  • Produce and distribute recruiting newsletters ("Ready to Serve" series)
  • Conduct recruiting events at partner institutions (UNT, TWU, high schools, fire academies)
  • Manage diversity recruitment initiatives

Administrative & Reporting

  • Submit weekly task reports to Fire Chief (auto-generated via Power Automate on Sundays)
  • Prepare quarterly Program Appraisals with full metrics, budget reconciliation, and appendices
  • Manage P-Card reconciliation and expense tracking across four budget accounts
  • Attend Monday Staff Meetings, CRR Meetings, Command Staff meetings, and Civil Service Commission meetings
