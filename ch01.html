Chapter 1

Denton Fire Department Overview

1.1 Department History and Mission

The Denton Fire Department (DFD) was founded in 1874 and has served the City of Denton continuously for over 150 years. Today, DFD provides an all-hazards approach model that includes response to fires, emergency medical services (EMS), hazardous materials response, and technical rescues. The department serves a population of approximately 166,000 residents across the City of Denton, Texas.

Mission Statement: The Denton Fire Department is a team of dedicated professionals who serve to protect lives and property in our community.

Vision Statement: The Denton Fire Department will be an innovative leader that proactively embraces the future while honoring the traditions of the fire service.

RICH Core Values

The Denton Fire Department operates under the RICH values framework, which serves as the organizational pillars:

  • Respect — Treating all individuals with dignity and consideration
  • Integrity — Maintaining honesty and strong moral principles in all actions
  • Courage — Demonstrating bravery in the face of adversity, both on and off the fireground
  • Humility — Serving others before self and maintaining a learner's mindset

1.2 Department Organization

The Denton Fire Department is led by Fire Chief Kenneth Hedges and operates from nine fire stations strategically positioned throughout the city. The department employs approximately 235 sworn personnel and support staff organized across several divisions.

Fire Stations

DFD operates from nine stations across Denton, including the historic "Powerhouse" Station 1, which in April 2025 became home to Texas' first electric fire truck. Fire Station 9 opened in the prior year, with ongoing renovations at Stations 5 and 6 continuing through 2025-2026.

1.3 Accreditations and Distinctions

The Denton Fire Department holds several significant national accreditations and ratings that reflect the quality of services provided:

ISO Class 1 Rating

Effective October 2025, the Denton Fire Department achieved an ISO (Insurance Services Office) Class 1 rating, placing it among the top 1% of fire departments nationwide. This rating directly impacts homeowner insurance premiums for Denton residents and reflects the department's commitment to excellence in fire suppression capability, emergency communications, water supply, and community risk reduction.

CAAS Accreditation

In August 2025, DFD achieved perfect-score accreditation from the Commission on Accreditation of Ambulance Services (CAAS), making it one of only 34 departments in the United States and Canada to hold this distinction. CAAS accreditation demonstrates compliance with rigorous standards for clinical quality, response time performance, and operational excellence in emergency medical services.

CPSE/CFAI Accreditation

The department participates in the Commission on Fire Accreditation International (CFAI) through the Commission on Professional Standards for Excellence (CPSE). This accreditation process involves comprehensive self-assessment, community risk assessment, and Standards of Cover documentation. The CRR Officer plays a direct role in supporting accreditation through program appraisals, data collection, and recommendation responses. (See Chapter 14 for detailed accreditation responsibilities.)

1.4 Department Programs Relevant to CRR

The CRR Officer interacts with or supports several department programs beyond the core CRR and Recruitment functions:

Emergency Management

The City of Denton Emergency Management division (Bob Martinez, Emergency Manager; Alyssa Owen, Deputy) coordinates the city's emergency management program across all four phases: Preparedness, Response, Recovery, and Mitigation. Emergency Management offers free community training including Prepare Denton, Stop the Bleed, and Active Threat Response. The CRR Officer should coordinate with Emergency Management on overlapping community outreach efforts and cross-promote training opportunities through CRR newsletters.

Fire Prevention / Fire Marshal's Office

The Fire Prevention division handles fire code enforcement, plan review, inspections, and fire investigations. Key systems include eTRAKiT for permits and inspections (no paper submittals accepted), The Compliance Engine for fire protection system deficiency tracking, and the requirement for 3rd party plan review on fire construction permits effective November 2024\. Fire Prevention staff are recipients of Water Shutoff Survey notifications for sprinkler system shutoffs. The CRR Officer maintains the Fire Watch Guidelines tool that supports Fire Prevention operations.

Free Smoke Detector Program

DFD has partnered with the American Red Cross to provide and install free smoke detectors within Denton city limits. Community members can complete an online form to request free detectors. In Q1 FY 25-26, the CRR Officer coordinated a smoke detector installation drive resulting in 50 detectors installed in homes that previously lacked working smoke detection. This program should be promoted through CRR newsletters and community events.

Community Wildfire Protection Plan (CWPP)

The Denton County CWPP is a multi-agency effort to identify areas with higher wildfire risk and develop mitigation strategies. While primarily managed through the Fire Marshal's Office, the CRR Officer should be aware of this plan and incorporate wildfire preparedness messaging into seasonal newsletters when appropriate.

1.5 Operating Environment

Fiscal Year Calendar

The City of Denton and DFD operate on an October 1 through September 30 fiscal year:

  • Q1: October – December
  • Q2: January – March
  • Q3: April – June
  • Q4: July – September

The current fiscal year is FY 25-26 (October 2025 through September 2030). All budget allocations, quarterly reports, and program appraisals follow this calendar. [UPDATE ANNUALLY]

Shift Structure

DFD Operations personnel work a 24-on/48-off shift rotation. The CRR Officer position is a day-shift administrative assignment operating on a "Flex On/Flex Off" schedule, which provides scheduling flexibility to accommodate evening and weekend recruiting events, community outreach activities, and other duties that fall outside standard business hours. This flexibility is essential given that many recruiting events, career fairs, and community events occur on evenings and weekends.

Reporting Chain

The CRR Officer reports through the following chain of command:

  1. CRR Officer (Captain Hunter Lott)
  1. Division Chief (Chief Skipper — CRA and operational oversight)
  1. Deputy Chief / Assistant Chief
  1. Fire Chief (Chief Kenneth Hedges — weekly reports submitted directly)

Weekly task reports are submitted directly to Chief Hedges. CRR meeting attendance and Community Risk Assessment (CRA) activities are coordinated with Chief Skipper.
