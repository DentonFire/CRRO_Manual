Chapter 9

The Hiring Process

The DFD hiring process follows a seven-step sequence governed by the Hiring Guidelines (Rev. 10/20/2021), Texas Local Government Code Chapter 143, the Local Rules, and the Meet and Confer Agreement. The CRR Officer manages this entire process end-to-end without dedicated HR support. Each step is detailed below, with particular emphasis on the credibility assessment (VerifEYE) and background investigation processes that were significantly updated in FY 25-26.

Step Phase Owner Key Actions
1 Civil Service Exam CRR \+ FPSI Written exam, scoring, eligibility list
2 Personal History Statement CRR Officer PHS distribution, collection, investigator assignment
3 Credibility Assessment CRR \+ VerifEYE Pre-Test Questionnaire, VerifEYE exam, results review
3B Background Investigation Investigators PHS verification, final investigation report
4 Physical Ability Test CRR \+ Truck 1 Timed physical assessment

9.1 Step 1: Civil Service Examination

Vendor: FPSI (Fire & Police Selection, Inc.)

Venue: University Gateway Center (or comparable venue)

The Civil Service Exam is a written knowledge examination administered annually. The exam is developed by FPSI and tests general aptitude, reading comprehension, and reasoning skills relevant to firefighting.

Pre-Exam Checklist

  1. Generate the Position Opportunity Announcement (POA) and present to Civil Service Commission for approval
  1. Secure venue (University Gateway Center)
  1. Order test booklets from FPSI
  1. Set up NEOGOV for candidate registration
  1. Distribute exam announcements via recruiting newsletter, Handshake, social media
  1. Send extra points reminder email to all registered candidates
  1. Hire OT proctor coverage for exam day
  1. Prepare slide show for exam day orientation
  1. Prepare 85+ instruction packets

Exam Day

  1. Arrive early for venue setup and registration table preparation
  1. Verify candidate identification
  1. Deliver orientation presentation (rules, prohibited items, timeline)
  1. Administer exam per FPSI protocols
  1. Collect all test booklets (critical: all booklets must be returned to FPSI)
  1. Generate raw score list
  1. Send follow-up instructions to candidates scoring 84+

Post-Exam

  1. Verify all extra points documentation (DD-214, residency proof, LaGrone certificate)
  1. Add verified preference points to raw scores
  1. Certify eligibility list
  1. Prepare and deliver demographics report to Chief and Civil Service Commission
  1. Return all test booklets to FPSI

9.2 Step 2: Personal History Statement (PHS)

Candidates who meet the score threshold receive the Personal History Statement packet. The PHS is a comprehensive background documentation package that candidates must complete and return by a firm deadline. The PHS form is hosted on Laserfiche:

https://lfpubweb.cityofdenton.com/Forms/rWi9N

PHS Administration

  1. Send PHS form link and instructions to all qualifying candidates via NEOGOV/email
  1. Set a firm deadline for PHS submission (typically 2-3 weeks after exam results)
  1. Process returned PHS packets in NEOGOV as they arrive
  1. Track submission status and follow up with candidates who have not returned packets
  1. Forward completed PHS packets to background investigators
  1. Incomplete packets or packets with obvious disqualifiers are forwarded to Assistant Chiefs for documentation

Key PHS Rules

  • No changes may be made to a PHS packet once it is accepted, either by the applicant or an agent of the Department
  • Packets determined to be incomplete or containing obvious disqualifiers are forwarded to Assistant Chiefs for DQ documentation
  • Candidates who fail to submit a PHS by the deadline are disqualified (Section 2, C.IV of the Hiring Guidelines)

9.3 Step 3: Credibility Assessment (VerifEYE)

In FY 25-26, the Denton Fire Department transitioned from traditional polygraph examinations to VerifEYE, a mobile app-based credibility assessment system developed by Converus. VerifEYE uses eye-tracking technology to measure pupil responses to true/false statements, replacing the traditional polygraph examiner and instrument with a phone-based test that takes approximately 10-15 minutes.

9.3.2 The Four Test Topics

The VerifEYE V4R test evaluates credibility across four relevant topics. These topics were reviewed and approved by DFD during initial configuration:

\# Topic Definition/Scope
1 Ties to Terrorism Domestic and foreign terrorism; includes specific group examples in test language
2 Illegal Drug Use (5 yr) Broad definition; includes marijuana regardless of medical card or state legality
3 Fired/Resigned in Lieu Terminated from any job, or resigned to avoid termination
4 Major Crime Theft \>$300, drug sales, assault, sexual offenses, fraud, arson, gang affiliation, etc.

9.3.3 Two Test Types

VerifEYE provides two distinct test versions. The CRR Officer determines which test to send each candidate based on their Pre-Test Questionnaire responses:

No Admissions Test (ID 3843): Used for candidates whose Pre-Test Questionnaire is clean across all four test categories. The credibility test verifies they are being truthful about having no relevant history. This is the baseline test and was approved first.

Admissions Test: Used for candidates who disclosed relevant history on their Pre-Test Questionnaire (drug use within the window, termination, criminal activity, etc.). This version is structured to account for their disclosures so the test does not flag things they already reported. The Admissions test was built by the vendor after the No Admissions version was approved.

9.3.4 DFD Pre-Test Questionnaire

Before any VerifEYE test is sent, every candidate completes the DFD Pre-Test Questionnaire. This is a DFD-branded fillable PDF that serves as the admissions capture tool, replacing the traditional polygraph pre-test interview. The questionnaire was developed in FY 25-26 specifically to align with the four VerifEYE test topics and the DFD Hiring Guidelines disqualifiers.

Section Title Coverage VerifEYE Alignment

2 Illegal Drug Use 11 questions \+ drug history table; includes marijuana (2 yr), hard drugs (5 yr), manufacture/delivery Topic 2: Drugs
3 Employment Termination Fired, resigned in lieu, asked to resign, walked off job Topic 3: Fired/Resigned

The questionnaire includes a certification and signature page where the candidate attests that all information is true and complete, and acknowledges that falsification may result in permanent disqualification.

9.3.5 VerifEYE Processing Guide

This section covers the end-to-end process for reviewing completed DFD Pre-Test Questionnaires, determining the correct VerifEye exam type (No Admissions vs. Admissions), generating test links through the VerifEye/Converus portal, and sending test link emails to candidates. This process occurs after candidates have passed the Personal History Statement (PHS) stage and submitted their Pre-Test Questionnaires.

Step 1: Collect Completed Pre-Test Questionnaires

Distribute the DFD Pre-Test Questionnaire (fillable PDF) to all candidates who passed the PHS stage. Collect completed, signed questionnaires. Verify each form includes:

  • Full legal name, date of birth, position, and date
  • All sections completed (Sections 1-10)
  • Signed and dated Certification on Page 14

Step 2: Review Each Questionnaire and Determine Exam Type

The VerifEye system offers two exam types. The correct exam is determined by whether the candidate disclosed relevant history in any of the four test topic categories:

Category No Admissions Criteria Triggers Admissions
Drug Use All drug use outside 5-year window OR no drug use at all Any illegal drug use within the past 5 years
Termination No firings, forced resignations, or asked to resign (Q1-Q3 in Section 3\) Fired, resigned to avoid termination, or asked to resign from any job
Criminal Activity No major criminal disclosures in Section 4 Major criminal activity disclosed (crimes against persons, arson, burglary, etc.)
Terrorism All No in Section 5 Any Yes in Section 5

Decision Rule: If the candidate has NO admissions across all four categories, assign the No Admissions test. If the candidate disclosed relevant history in any one of the four categories, assign the Admissions test.

Important Notes on Exam Type Determination:

  • Minor workplace write-ups (e.g., uniform violations, cash register errors, tardiness) do NOT trigger Admissions unless they involved a termination (Q1-Q3)
  • Quitting without two weeks' notice (Q8) is NOT a termination category hit
  • Marijuana use outside the 5-year window (e.g., last use 2019 when testing in 2026\) does NOT trigger Admissions
  • Minor petty theft or shoplifting alone does NOT typically trigger Admissions unless combined with other criminal disclosures
  • Watch for inconsistencies between sections (e.g., candidate mentions "marijuana arrests" in Section 9 but discloses no criminal history in Section 4). Flag these for the pre-test interview

Step 3: Generate Test Links in the VerifEye Portal

  1. Log into the VerifEye/Converus Dashboard (see Appendix G.2 for login procedure)
  1. Navigate to the VerifEye tab and click "Create Test Link"
  1. Select "New Examinee" and enter the candidate's full name and email address
  1. Under Test Name, select the appropriate exam type (No Admissions or Admissions) based on your Step 2 review
  1. Click Submit. The portal generates a unique URL for that candidate
  1. Click "Copy Link" to copy the URL to your clipboard

Important: Each candidate receives their own unique link. Never reuse or share links between candidates.

Step 4: Email the Test Link to the Candidate

Send the following email from the CRR Officer's City email address. Paste the VerifEye link after the sign-off at the bottom of the email.

Email Template
Subject: VerifEye Test To: [Candidate email address] [First Name], Below is your test link. Make sure you follow the instructions completely and listen carefully. The test will ask you various forms of the same question so, if you get one or two messed up, it doesn’t mean you fail. Respectfully, Captain [Last Name] [PASTE VERIFEYE LINK HERE]

The reassuring language about getting one or two questions wrong is intentional and helps reduce candidate anxiety. Do not modify the email template. See also Appendix F.1 for additional template notes.

Step 5: Track and Monitor Results

  • Results are delivered through the VerifEye dashboard once the candidate completes the test
  • Review results: advance passing candidates, DQ failing candidates
  • Move passing candidates to the PAT stage in NEOGOV
  • Do not notify candidates that they are advancing until the background investigators confirm they have completed their final investigation. Moving candidates in NEOGOV is an internal tracking action, not a candidate notification

When sending the VerifEYE test link, communicate the following requirements to candidates:

An interruption (incoming call, notification, etc.) can corrupt the test and require a restart. Emphasize this to candidates in the test link email.

9.3.7 Proctored Administration (Optional)

The CRR Officer may also administer the test in person using a tripod setup. The V3R/V4R Pretest Checklist provides the step-by-step script for proctored administration. Key considerations for proctored tests: verify the signed consent form, confirm no restroom breaks needed, check for eye obstructions, position the candidate leaning over a desk with the phone on a tripod at mouth level looking approximately 2 inches above the camera, and warn about the camera flash.

9.3.8 Test Type Determination Guide

Use the following decision framework when reviewing Pre-Test Questionnaires to determine which VerifEYE test to assign:

Fired or resigned in lieu of termination Admissions Direct hit on Topic 3
Major criminal activity (theft \>$300, assault, etc.) Admissions Direct hit on Topic 4
Minor petty theft only (under thresholds) No Admissions Below Major Crime threshold
Inconsistencies between questionnaire sections Admissions Deeper probe warranted

9.4 Step 3B: Background Investigation

While the VerifEYE credibility assessment is processing, background investigators conduct a parallel final investigation to verify the information provided in each candidate's PHS. The investigation runs concurrently with or immediately following the poly process.

9.4.1 Investigation Team

Background investigations are conducted by assigned investigators (typically Captains) who review all aspects of the candidate's personal history. For the FY 25-26 hiring cycle, investigators included:

  • Captain Matt Roberts (5A)
  • Captain Jeb Miller
  • Captain Ward Morton

Stacy Fruge provides administrative support for file organization in Laserfiche.

9.4.2 Investigation Process

  1. CRR Officer distributes PHS packets to assigned investigators
  1. CRR Officer requests Stacy Fruge organize the Poly results and Pre-Test Questionnaire responses into each candidate's Laserfiche file
  1. Investigators verify all PHS information through their final investigation
  1. Investigators prepare their investigation report and attach it to the top of the printed PHS
  1. Investigators include any notes they consider important for the Chiefs to review during interviews
  1. If an investigator determines a candidate should be disqualified, they move the candidate to the "Disqualification Letter" stage in NEOGOV and share their notes with the CRR Officer
  1. Once all final investigations are complete, someone delivers all packets to the CRR Officer's office
  1. CRR Officer locks all packets in a secure cabinet until interviews

9.4.3 Coordination with NEOGOV Stages

As candidates complete their VerifEYE exam, the CRR Officer moves them to the "PAT" stage in the NEOGOV workflow. This is an internal tracking action only. Candidates are not notified they are advancing until the investigators confirm all final investigations are complete. This prevents a situation where a candidate is told they passed and later gets disqualified based on investigation findings.

If an investigator DQs a candidate during the investigation, the investigator (or the CRR Officer at their direction) moves that candidate to the "Disqualification Letter" stage in NEOGOV. The CRR Officer then generates the appropriate DQ letter.

9.5 Step 4: Physical Ability Test (PAT)

The Physical Ability Test is a timed physical assessment of candidate fitness for firefighting duties. The CRR Officer manages all PAT logistics including equipment procurement, venue coordination, staffing, preparation programs, and results documentation. The PAT is administered per the Denton Fire Department Physical Ability Test Instructor Guide.

9.5.1 PAT Course Events

The DFD PAT consists of a stair climb (Event 1) followed by five sequential events (Events 2–6). Candidates wear a 50 lb weighted vest throughout. During Event 1, candidates also wear two 12.5 lb shoulder weights. Event 1 and Events 2–6 are timed separately. Remaining time from Event 1 does not carry over to Events 2–6.

EventTime LimitDescription
Event 14:00Stair Climb: 3-story stairwell to 4th floor landing, 3 times. 50 lb vest + two 12.5 lb shoulder weights.
Rest Period1:30 maxBetween Event 1 and Events 2–6. Transfer shoulder weights to proctor; retain 50 lb vest.
Event 2—Ladder Raise & Extension: Walk 24ft ladder hand-over-hand, then extend fly section via halyard.
Event 3—Equipment Carry: Remove two saws, carry 75ft, 180° turn, carry back, replace in compartment.
Event 4—1-3/4″ Hose Drag: Advance charged hose line, then pull 50ft into marked area from one knee.
Event 5—Sledge Swing: Swing sledge in chopping motion to move weighted object ~5 feet.
Event 6—Rescue: Drag ~165 lb mannequin 35ft, 180° around drum, 35ft back to finish.
Events 2–6 Total5:30Continuous clock across all five events including walking between stations.

PPE Note: Candidates wear department-issued helmets and 50 lb weighted vests. Candidates may bring their own gloves; thin gardening or cotton gloves are not permitted. Only one small vest is available, so the waiting line instructor may need to adjust candidate order based on sizing. If a candidate's helmet falls off during an event, allow them to finish the current event before having them put it back on.

9.6 Step 5: Panel Interviews

Candidates who pass the PAT advance to panel interviews conducted by Chief-level officers. The CRR Officer coordinates:

  • Interview scheduling with interview panel members and candidates
  • Interview room setup and logistics
  • Interview process development (coordinated with Chief Skipper)
  • Score compilation and ranking

Before interviews, the CRR Officer provides each panel member with the candidate's PHS packet (with the investigator's report attached on top) from the secure cabinet.

9.7 Step 6: Conditional Letter of Employment

Candidates selected through the interview process receive a Conditional Letter of Employment contingent on successful completion of:

  • Medical examination \-- Vendor: Concentra (\~$825/exam). Account: 320100.7726 (Physicals/Psych)
  • Psychological evaluation \-- Vendor: Pepper & Associates (\~$215/exam). Account: 320100.7726
  • Verification of required certifications

The CRR Officer generates conditional letters, coordinates medical and psych exam scheduling with vendors, and tracks completion status.

Candidates who fail the psychological assessment have the right to appeal under TLGC Chapter 143.022(c) by requesting a board of three psychologists (candidate pays). The board's decision is final.

9.8 Step 7: Appointment Letter

Upon successful completion of all conditional requirements, the candidate receives a formal Appointment Letter with their start date and onboarding instructions. The CRR Officer generates the appointment letter and coordinates the start date, typically targeting a specific pay period.

In Q1 FY 25-26, 6 firefighters were processed through this complete hiring pipeline.

9.9 Disqualification Process

At any stage in the hiring process, candidates who do not meet requirements receive a formal disqualification letter. The CRR Officer generates all DQ letters. This is a legal requirement under Texas Local Government Code Section 143.026(c), which states that the Chief Executive "shall clearly set forth in writing the good and sufficient reason why the person having the highest grade was not appointed."

9.9.1 DQ Letter Format

All disqualification letters follow a standard format using City of Denton letterhead:

  1. City of Denton logo and department header
  1. Date and candidate mailing address
  1. Opening paragraph thanking the candidate for their interest
  1. Disqualifier section identifying the specific reason(s)
  1. Hiring Guidelines reference citing the applicable section and subsection
  1. Texas LGC 143.026(c) language regarding written notice
  1. Closing paragraph with contact information for questions
  1. Chief Kenneth Hedges signature block

9.9.2 Common Disqualification Categories

The DFD Hiring Guidelines (Rev. 10/20/2021) Section 11 defines the disqualifier framework. The most commonly used sections are:

Section Category Scope Duration
11.A Contradictory Info Falsification, omission, or inaccurate reporting Permanent (intentional) or eligibility period (unintentional)
11.B Credit/Financial 90+ day delinquency, child support, fraud Eligibility period
11.C Criminal Behavior Felony (permanent); Class A Misd (\<5 yr); Class B Misd (\<1 yr); listed offenses incl. prostitution Varies by offense
11.D Driving 18+ points on scale, suspended license \<24 mo Eligibility period
11.E Drugs Manufacture/delivery (permanent); marijuana \<2 yr; hard drugs \<5 yr; positive drug screen (permanent) Varies by substance

9.9.4 Record Keeping

Maintain records of all disqualifications for Civil Service Commission reporting. Store copies of all DQ letters in the candidate's file. The CRR Officer tracks all DQ decisions in NEOGOV and can generate reports as needed. DQ letters are typically dated and signed by the Fire Chief.
