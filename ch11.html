Chapter 11

Special Programs and Future Initiatives

11.1 Citizens Fire Academy (CFA)

The Citizens Fire Academy is a multi-week educational program for community members. Feasibility assessment for resuming the CFA is ongoing. The CRR Officer coordinates curriculum development, guest speakers, facilities, and participant logistics.

11.2 Explorer/Scout Program

Partnership with Boy Scouts of America for youth fire service education. BSA registration was renewed in Q1 FY 25-26 ($66.95/instructor). Requires an instructor with current BSA registration and safety training certifications.

11.3 Clown Program

Fire prevention clown program was inactive in Q1 FY 25-26. Requires identifying and training performers if the program resumes. This is a succession planning need.

11.4 Fire Camp

Annual youth fire camp program. Status: TBD for FY 25-26. Requires coordination for purchasing, staffing, meals, school permissions, and partner involvement.

11.5 Smoke Detector Program

Community smoke detector installation program. In Q1 FY 25-26, 50 smoke detectors were installed in community residences. Track installations through ESO for quarterly reporting.

11.6 Hoarding Awareness Campaign

Video-based community education campaign on hoarding dangers. Campaign launched January-February 2026 with MarComm video content production. Track engagement metrics through social media analytics.

11.7 DFR/UAS Program

Drone as First Responder (DFR) and Unmanned Aircraft System (UAS) operations are a shared responsibility with the Public Safety UAS program. Nova coordinates the technical program development. The CRR Officer supports:

  • Pilot training coordination and FAA Part 107 certification tracking
  • DFR capability expansion (Station 7\)
  • DroneSense reporting and operational management
  • UAS monthly training coordination

11.8 Succession Planning Needs

Several programs require succession planning beyond the CRR Officer position. These are documented in Chapter 17 (System Administration & Succession Planning).
