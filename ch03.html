Chapter 3

Key Relationships and Partners

3.1 Internal Stakeholders

Fire Chief's Office

The CRR Officer submits weekly task reports directly to Chief Hedges. Quarterly Program Appraisals are also routed through the Chief's office. The Chief's office provides strategic direction on department priorities that affect CRR and recruitment messaging. The hiring and termination report is prepared for the Chief as needed.

Division Chief (Chief Skipper)

Chief Skipper provides direct operational oversight for CRR activities. Regular CRA (Community Risk Assessment) meetings are held to align CRR programs with identified community risks. The CRR Officer coordinates with Chief Skipper on interview process development, UAS program direction, and CRR strategic priorities.

Fire Prevention Division

Fire Prevention receives automated notifications from the Water Shutoff Survey when sprinkler system shutoffs are reported. The CRR Officer maintains the Fire Watch Guidelines tool that supports Fire Prevention operations during fire protection system impairments. Coordinate with Fire Prevention on After the Fire resources and building safety information (see the Building Safety reconstruction steps document).

Operations Division

On-duty Operations crews support CRR events when available within their districts, though dedicated overtime for CRR activities was discontinued due to budget constraints in 2025\. Operations personnel use the Water Shutoff Survey tool in the field and should be trained on its use. The CRR Officer coordinates with specific companies for PAT preparation help (e.g., Truck 1 for Practice PAT support).

Civil Service Commission

The CRR Officer provides deliverables to the Civil Service Commission including the Position Opportunity Announcement, certified eligibility lists, demographics reports, and communications about upcoming meetings. Commission meetings require attendance and prepared materials.

MarComm (Marketing & Communications)

DFD MarComm supports CRR and Recruiting through collaborative content creation (Veterans Day campaigns, video script development), social media coordination, and brand consistency. The CRR Officer coordinates with MarComm for major campaigns and media inquiry responses.

3.2 External Partners

University of North Texas (UNT)

UNT is the primary university recruitment partner. The CRR Officer maintains a Handshake account for posting positions and events. Key programs include ELEVATE Athlete recruiting events, career fairs, and campus presentations. UNT has also been a partner for media content (e.g., battery safety interview). Maintain regular contact with UNT career services and the ELEVATE program coordinator.

Texas Woman's University (TWU)

TWU is a secondary recruitment partner. The CRR Officer attends TWU career fairs and recruitment events. Coordinate with TWU career services for event scheduling.

Local High Schools

Career day presentations are conducted at local high schools including Guyer High School, Calhoun Middle School, and Strickland Middle School. These events introduce students to fire service careers and serve both CRR education and long-term recruitment pipeline development.

Fire Academies

Recruit from fire academies including LaGrone Advanced Technology Center (whose graduates receive \+1 preference point on the Civil Service exam), North Central Texas College (NCTC), and Weatherford College. Academy visits should be scheduled quarterly during active recruiting cycles.

FPSI (Fire & Police Selection, Inc.)

FPSI is the contracted vendor for Civil Service exam administration. They provide test materials (booklets), scoring, and study guide resources. The CRR Officer is responsible for executing the FPSI contract, coordinating exam logistics, and ensuring timely return of test booklets after each exam. Study guides are available through firepolicejobs.com.

VerifEYE

VerifEYE was selected in FY 25-26 as the replacement polygraph vendor, offering remote testing capability through a mobile app version, enhanced accuracy, and cost savings compared to traditional polygraph methods. The CRR Officer coordinates onboarding, scheduling, and contract management with VerifEYE.

Boy Scouts of America (BSA)

The Fire Explorer program is managed in coordination with BSA. Annual dues ($66.95 per instructor), scout safety training (3 hours), and membership renewals are managed by the CRR Officer. The Explorer program was successfully renewed in Q1 FY 25-26.

Constant Contact

The email marketing platform used for both CRR and Recruiting newsletters. The CRR Officer manages subscriber lists, creates newsletter content, and tracks analytics (open rates, click-through rates, unsubscribe rates). As of Q1 FY 25-26, the CRR newsletter serves 471 subscribers and the Recruiting newsletter reaches 3,960 subscribers.

Subtext

A text messaging platform finalized for setup in Q1 FY 25-26. Subtext enables direct text message communication with community members and candidates. [ACTION REQUIRED] Verify current status of Subtext deployment and subscriber onboarding.
