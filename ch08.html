Chapter 8

Civil Service Framework

8.1 Legal Governance

Firefighter hiring at the Denton Fire Department is governed by Texas Local Government Code Chapter 143, which establishes the Civil Service framework for fire departments in cities with populations over 10,000. This framework creates specific requirements for testing, eligibility lists, hiring order, and candidate rights that differ significantly from standard municipal HR processes.

Key implications for the CRR Officer:

  • The hiring process operates under Civil Service rules, not standard HR procedures
  • NEOGOV hiring workflow is managed independently by the CRR Officer, without dedicated HR support
  • Candidate communications, eligibility lists, and hiring decisions must comply with Chapter 143 requirements
  • The Civil Service Commission provides oversight and must approve the Position Opportunity Announcement (POA)
  • The Meet and Confer Agreement between DFD and the firefighter association may contain additional provisions affecting hiring

8.2 Position Details

Element Detail
Position Title Firefighter
Starting Salary $86,022 annually \+ certification pay
Salary with Cert Pay Up to $101,337.60 (depending on certifications held)
Minimum Age 18 years old
Driver's License Valid Texas driver's license required
Education High school diploma or GED
Certifications (Preferred) TCFP Structural Firefighter, EMT/Paramedic

8.3 Preference Points

Under the DFD Civil Service rules and Meet and Confer Agreement, eligible candidates may receive preference points added to their raw exam score:

Preference Points Verification
Military Service \+5 DD-214 (Member 4 copy)
Denton Residency \+1 Proof of Denton address (utility bill, lease, etc.)
LaGrone Academy Graduate \+1 LaGrone certificate of completion

Extra points are verified post-exam by the CRR Officer, added to raw scores, and the adjusted scores are used to generate the certified eligibility list. Send a reminder email about extra points documentation to all registered candidates before exam day.

8.4 Tie-Breaking Criteria

When two or more candidates attain the same final score, ties are broken in the following order:

  1. Raw score (before preference points)
  1. TCFP Structural Firefighter certification
  1. EMT-Paramedic (EMT-P) certification or license
  1. EMT-Basic (EMT-B) certification
  1. Bachelor's degree
  1. Earliest registration date
